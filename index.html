Beginner

The Staff: The 5 lines and 4 spaces where notes are placed.

Treble Clef: Used for high-pitched instruments like the violin or flute.

Tip: Spaces spell FACE. Lines are Every Good Boy Does Fine.

Intermediate

Ledger Lines: Extra lines drawn above or below the staff for very high or low notes.

Accidentals: Sharps (♯) raise a note by a half-step; Flats (♭) lower it.

Advanced

Bass Clef: Used for low-pitched instruments like the cello or tuba.

Key Signatures: A set of sharps or flats at the beginning of a piece that tells you which notes to change throughout.
