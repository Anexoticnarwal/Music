Intermediate: Intervals

Instead of reading every note, pianists look for Intervals (distances). A 2nd is the next white key, a 3rd skips one.

  • 2nd: Adjacent notes (C to D)
  • 3rd: Skip one note (C to E)
  • 4th: Skip two notes (C to F)
  • 5th: Skip three notes (C to G)
💡 Tip: Use your fingers to count on the staff. Put one finger on each line and space to keep track of intervals.

Advanced: Reading Faster

Professional musicians use these techniques to sight-read quickly:

  • Chunking: Group notes into patterns rather than reading individually
  • Hand Position: Your hand naturally covers an octave—use this reference point
  • Look Ahead: Always scan 1-2 measures ahead while playing
  • Anchor Notes: Memorize a few landmarks (like Middle C) to navigate faster
💡 Tip: Practice reading without playing. Your eyes should always be slightly ahead of your fingers.

Bass Clef Basics

The Bass Clef (left hand) uses a different notation system:

  • Lines (bottom to top): G, B, D, F, A
  • Spaces (bottom to top): A, C, E, G
  • Memory aid: "Good Boys Do Fine Always" for lines, "All Cows Eat Grass" for spaces
💡 Tip: Bass clef is just treble clef moved down. Middle C appears on the ledger line above the bass staff.

Practice Tips

  • Start slow—accuracy before speed
  • Practice one clef at a time before mixing them
  • Use the Practice tab daily for 5-10 minutes
  • Write out note names on sheet music to reinforce memory
  • Challenge yourself: take a real piece and name every note before playing
